This preference includes the depiction of information in maps, spider diagrams, charts, graphs, flow charts, labelled diagrams, and all the symbolic arrows, circles, hierarchies and other devices, that people use to represent what could have been presented in words.
It does NOT include still pictures or photographs of reality,movies, videos or PowerPoint. It does include designs, whitespace, patterns, shapes and the different formats that are used to highlight and convey information. Alongside the fulfilment of the BSc (Hons)
in Computer Science programme requirements, the module content serves as a foundation to build a successful career in cybersecurity. Generally, the knowledge gained will be useful in securely performing regular business activities involving information systems.
Learning activities in this module include regular lectures, practical workshops, group and individual assignments, and a final exam. Students are sincerely advised to refer to the module handbook as often as required to understand the nature of the module and all
the learning activities included to gain the most out of the module.
